Our Team

Gary Andersen, PhD, Founder & Chair, Scientific Advisory Board
Gary is a scientist at Lawrence Berkeley National Labs and Group Leader for Molecular and Microbial Ecology; he is an inventor of the PhyloChip™ technology and leader of the GreenGenes database team, which provides access to 16s rRNA prokaryotic multiple sequence alignment. Gary developed the strain identification system for anthrax at Duke University. His PhD project at UC Berkeley involved testing Ice Minus bacteria for crop protection. He earned his BS in Biology from Northwestern University.

Todd DeSantis, Co-Founder & Head of Bioinformatics
Todd was chief software designer for multiple16S rRNA applications including the GreenGenes database, the PhyloChip design software and the PhyloChip analysis algorithms. Todd manages all bioinformatics work for every project and oversees the planning, performance and interpretation of the customers projects and the production of meaningful data reports.

Peter Gardner, Board Member
Peter is a Managing Director with Wavepoint Ventures. He brings 16 years of professional venture investing experience and has served on the boards of numerous early-stage technology companies. Before entering the venture capital industry Peter was a project leader at Weston Solutions, a national environmental engineering and consulting company. Peter has an MBA from The Anderson School at UCLA and bachelor’s degrees in both Biology and Environmental Science from Tufts University.

Peter Gleick, PhD, Board Member
Peter is co-founder and president of the Pacific Institute for Studies in Development, Environment, and Security in Oakland, California. He is an internationally recognized water expert and was named a MacArthur Fellow in October 2003 for his work. In 2001, Peter was dubbed a "visionary on the environment" by the British Broadcasting Corporation. In 1999, he was elected an Academician of the International Water Academy, in Oslo, Norway and in 2006, he was elected to the National Academy of Sciences, Washington, D.C. Peter received a B.S. from Yale University, and an M.S. and Ph.D. from the University of California, Berkeley. His most recent book is Bottled and Sold: The Story Behind Our Obsession with Bottled Water.

Corey S. Goodman, PhD, Founder & Chairman of the Board
Corey was former President of Pfizer’s Biotherapeutics and Bioinnovation Center; co-founder Exelixis and Renovis, and CEO of Renovis. He is currently Managing Director and co-founder, venBio LLC; Professor at UCSF; Chairman of the Board: Limerick, iPierian, and Oligasis; member of Board of NeuroTherapeutics and Mirna. Corey is an elected member of the National Academy of Sciences. He was formerly a faculty member at UC Berkeley and Stanford and a Howard Hughes Medical Institute Investigator. Corey has won numerous awards and prizes for academic leadership and entrepreneurial activities.

John Kao, MD, Board Member & Strategic Advisor
John is an authority on the intersecting subjects of corporate innovation and transformation, design, and the future of business. Dubbed a “serial innovator” and “Mr. Creativity” by The Economist, he has made a career out of helping organizations go from “getting” the importance of innovation to “getting innovation done.” In addition to his other accomplishments, John is an entrepreneur, best-selling author, Tony-nominated producer, and business pioneer. For 14 years, he taught at Harvard Business School, and has also been a visiting professor at the MIT Media Lab as well as Distinguished Visiting Professor of Innovation at the U.S. Naval Postgraduate School in Monterey. John's most recent book on the global dynamics of innovation is called Innovation Nation: How America Is Losing Its Innovation Edge, Why It Matters and How We Can Get It Back.

Thane Kreiner, PhD, Founder, President & CEO
Thane founded and served as the start-up CEO of Presage Biosciences, Inc., where he built the team, licensed the IP, and closed the Series A financing. Thane was also start-up CEO of iZumi, Inc., (now iPierian), securing foundational intellectual property in the iPS field for the firm, raising the Series A, and forming a partnership with The Gladstone Institutes. For 14 years prior to that, Thane was a senior executive at Affymetrix, Inc., a pioneer of genetic information technologies, in various leadership roles including Senior Vice President, Marketing & Sales, Corporate Affairs and Business Operations. Thane serves on the Advisory Board for the Markkula Center for Applied Ethics. He earned his PhD in Neurosciences and his MBA from Stanford University.

Janet Warrington PhD, Co-Founder & Senior Vice-President, Operations and Development
Janet has more than 20 years in biotechnology and molecular diagnostics R&D experience and helped build Affymetrix, serving as VP of R&D, Molecular Diagnostics. In partnership with Roche Molecular Systems, she led the first FDA clearance of a microarray system and is a leader in driving establishment of standard controls and best practices, serving as work group co-chair for the development of Electronic Health Records standards under the auspices of the US Office of the Secretary of Health and Human Services and on the Clinical and Laboratory Standards Institute (CLSI), Molecular Methods Area Committee. Janet earned her PhD in Biological Chemistry from UC Irvine, and was a DOE supported postdoctoral scholar in genetics at Stanford University.

Matt Winkler, PhD, Board Member
Matt is CEO of Asuragen, Inc. He has a B.S. degree in Genetics and a Ph.D. in Zoology from the University of California at Berkeley and was an Associate Professor of Zoology Department of the University of Texas from 1983-1993. In 1988 he started Ambion, Inc., “The RNA Company” a molecular biology tool company. Ambion became the preeminent molecular biology tools company focusing on RNA, with about 400 employees. In March of 2006, he sold the research products division of Ambion to Applied Biosystems and with about 100 employees started Asuragen, a molecular diagnostics company with a strong focus around RNA in general and miRNA in particular. In December of 2007, Asuragen created Mirna Therapeutics, as a carve-out of Asuragen to develop cancer therapeutics based on miRNA.
