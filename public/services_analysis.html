Unique analysis exploits one-to-many relationships

Third-generation Technology

PhyloChip assays and algorithms are in their third generation of development, having been optimized through focused efforts at LBNL. Using over 1.1 million probes representing over 50,000 bacteria and archaea, our assay and algorithms are designed to leverage the one-to-many and many–to-many relationships that exist between probes and taxa. Full-length 16S sequences are queried to power confidence in data interpretation. The rich representation of full-length 16S sequence has been demonstrated as a powerful approach for profiling complex bacterial populations in a wide range of environmental, animal, and human sample types and matrices. Reference sequences are built into each and every assay for quality management and bench marking quantification.

Sensitivity and Specificity

We’ve become aware that it is challenging to assay all known bacterial species by pyrosequencing. Recently published reports of artifacts and anomalies with these methods describe problems linked to data quality assumptions. A combination of an unacceptable high frequency of errors in base calling and artifacts in taxonomic placement leads to inaccurate and non-reproducible descriptions of community composition. Validation studies have revealed irregularities in determination of relative abundance. Furthermore, there is a concern over the incidence of human subject DNA contaminating sequence reads and inadvertent publication of personal DNA, violating protection of patient privacy. Moreover, sufficiently deep sequencing, to enable detection of lower abundance bacteria and archaea, appears to limit the number of samples that can be analyzed, thereby dramatically reducing the power of statistical analyses for discernment of microbial patterns under various human and environmental conditions.
